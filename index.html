CHAPTER 3 · NEGOTIATING MEDICAL BILLS

Most bills are negotiable. Almost nobody asks.

This is the most overlooked money skill in America. A study from the Journal of the American Medical Association found that most patients who received a hospital bill did not know it was negotiable. Another study found that up to 80 percent of hospital bills contain billing errors.

Before you pay any large medical bill, do four things in order. First: request an itemized bill. Audit it for errors. Second: apply for financial assistance. Every nonprofit hospital is required to have a program, and most do not advertise it. Third: ask for the cash-pay or settlement rate. Fourth: only after the first three, set up a payment plan if you must.

Margaret's bill. Margaret received a hospital bill for $9,200 after a procedure. She requested an itemized bill and found $800 in duplicate charges she was able to dispute. The corrected bill was $8,400. She applied for financial assistance and did not qualify. She called billing and offered to pay $4,500 today as a full settlement. They accepted. Her total savings: $4,700 from a bill she almost just paid in full.

Margaret was not exceptional. She was simply willing to make four phone calls she had been told, implicitly, were not hers to make. That is the entire skill.
